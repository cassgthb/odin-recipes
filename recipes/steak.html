Filet Mignon Steak

Description from Wikipedia:

Filet mignon in North America, especially the United States refers solely to a cut of meat taken from the smaller end of a beef tenderloin, or psoas major of a cow. In France, filet mignon usually refers to cuts of pork tenderloin or veal tenderloin.

Ingredients:

  • Filet Mignon Steaks
    • Look for a deep red color free of an silver skin (connective tissue)
    • See if the steaks are soft to the touch
  • Steak seasoning
    • Generous amount of salt and pepper
  • Basting butter

Steps:

  1. Use a cast iron pan for a good sear
    • Don't use a non-stick pan
  2. Prep the steaks
    • Remove the steaks from the fridge about 30-40 minutes before cooking
    • Generously season all sides with salt and pepper and pat it in
  3. Sear the steaks
    • Heat the pan on the stove over high heat
    • Once the pan is very hot, add the oil and wait for it to shimmer before adding the steaks
    • Sear on one side for 2 minutes until they have a golden crust
    • Roll them on the sides for another minute to brown the edges
  4. Add the butter baste
    • Flip steaks to their final side and add butter, garlic, and rosemary
    • Butter baste the steaks with a spoon for 1 minute
  5. Bake the steaks
    • Transfer hot pan to the oven and bake for an addition 4-10 minutes
  6. Cover the steaks
    • Transfer the steaks onto a plate and rest them for 5 minutes before serving

Steak temperature tips:

  • Rare: 115ºF (3-4 minutes), rested temp 120ºF-125ºF
  • Medium Rare: 120ºF (5-6 minutes), rested temp 120ºF-130ºF
  • Medium: 130ºF (6-7 minutes), rested temp 135ºF-140ºF
  • Medium Well: 140ºF (8-9 minutes), rested temp 145ºF-150ºF
  • Well Done: 150ºF (10-11 minutes), rested temp 155ºF-160ºF
